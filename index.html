🍜 Restaurant & Cafe Guide

Ratings from Tabelog (Japan's top review site, score 3.5+ = excellent) and Google Maps. Click links to read full reviews.

Must Try
Mizuno (美津の)
みずの — Est. 1945
Okonomiyaki 📍 Dotonbori ¥1,200–1,800
3.58Tabelog 4.3Google
What reviewers say: Legendary 80-year-old okonomiyaki institution. The yamaimo-yaki (mountain yam) creates an impossibly fluffy texture. Often a 30-60 min queue but it moves fast. Counter seats let you watch masters at work. "Best okonomiyaki I've ever had" is a common refrain.
💡 Order the Yamaimo-yaki Mix (yam + seafood). Go before 11:30 or after 14:00 to avoid peak queue.
📖 Tabelog 📍 Google Maps
Creo-Ru (くれおーる)
くれおーる道頓堀店
Takoyaki 📍 Dotonbori ¥600–800
3.17Tabelog 4.0Google
What reviewers say: Solid takoyaki option right on the canal. Crispy outside, creamy inside. You can get both takoyaki and okonomiyaki here. It's a tourist-friendly spot with English menus and outdoor seating. Not the most "local" pick, but reliably good and convenient.
📖 Tabelog 📍 Google Maps
Must Try
Kushikatsu Daruma (だるま)
串かつだるま — Est. 1929
Kushikatsu 📍 Shinsekai ¥1,000–2,000
3.46Tabelog 4.2Google
What reviewers say: The most iconic kushikatsu chain in Osaka, almost 100 years old. The deep-fried skewers are light and perfectly crispy. The angry chef mascot is a Shinsekai landmark. Reviewers love the fun atmosphere and the strict "NO double-dipping in the sauce!" rule. Great value.
💡 Try the set course for variety. The Shinsekai main branch has the best atmosphere.

Ichiran Ramen
一蘭 道頓堀店
Ramen 📍 Dotonbori ¥980
3.28Tabelog 4.3Google
What reviewers say: Famous for individual booths — you eat alone with a curtain, focused purely on ramen. Customize spice, richness, noodle firmness via a form. The tonkotsu broth is creamy and consistent. Japanese locals consider it "decent chain ramen," but tourists love the unique experience. Long queues at peak hours.
💡 Go late night (open 24h at Dotonbori) to avoid queues.

Rikuro Ojisan (りくろーおじさん)

🍬 Sweets & Snacks
Kit Kat Japan Exclusives

Region-limited flavors you can't get abroad: matcha, strawberry cheesecake, sake, wasabi, Kobe pudding, roasted tea. The Tokyo Banana Kit Kat is a hit.

Rikuro Ojisan Cheesecake
Osaka Icon
Osaka's legendary jiggly cheesecake — wobbly, light, and only ¥965. Buy on your last day; best eaten fresh. The queues are part of the experience!
📍 Namba station branch, Namba main shop
💰 ¥965 per cake
Osaka Takoyaki Snacks
Classic Omiyage
Takoyaki-flavored senbei crackers, takoyaki-shaped cookies, and takoyaki-flavor Pretz. Fun, affordable, and unmistakably Osaka.
📍 Station kiosks, souvenir floors at dept. stores
💰 ¥500–1,000
Yatsuhashi (八ツ橋)
Kyoto Classic
Kyoto's #1 souvenir — soft cinnamon mochi triangles filled with red bean, matcha, strawberry, or chocolate. "Nama" (raw) version is the tastiest. Shogoin brand is the most famous.
📍 Kyoto Station, Nishiki Market, Kiyomizu-dera shops
💰 ¥500–1,000
Royce Chocolate
Fan Favorite
Hokkaido's famous Nama Chocolate — rich, ganache-like squares that melt on the tongue. Matcha and original are top sellers. Keep refrigerated! Available in Kansai Airport.
📍 KIX Airport, dept. store food halls
💰 ¥800–1,500
Tokyo Banana (東京ばな奈)
Japan's most gifted omiyage — banana-cream-filled sponge cakes. Limited seasonal flavors at airports. Yes, it's "Tokyo" but sold everywhere in Kansai Airport too.
📍 KIX Airport departure gates
💰 ¥600–1,200
Baton d'Or (バトンドール)
Osaka Only
Glico's premium Pocky — thicker, richer, and only sold in a few stores in Japan. Butter, matcha, and strawberry flavors. Luxurious packaging makes it a perfect gift.
📍 Hankyu Umeda, Takashimaya Namba basement
💰 ¥500–1,000
Kobe Pudding
Kobe Only
Custard pudding made with Rokko mountain milk. Creamy, smooth, and the jar is cute enough to reuse. Tōraku brand is the original and best.
📍 Kobe Sannomiya station, Harborland shops
💰 ¥400–600 each
🍵 Matcha & Tea
Uji Matcha Powder (Premium)
Premium Gift
Ceremonial-grade matcha from Uji — the birthplace of Japanese green tea. Nakamura Tokichi and Itohkyuemon brands are top-tier. Great for home tea ceremonies or baking.
📍 Nakamura Tokichi (Uji), Itohkyuemon (Uji)
💰 ¥1,000–3,000
Matcha Sweets Assortment
Safe Gift
Matcha chocolates, matcha langues de chat, matcha KitKats, matcha cookies. Available in beautifully packaged boxes. Tsujiri and Itohkyuemon make excellent gift boxes.
📍 Tsujiri shops, Uji souvenir stores, Nishiki Market
💰 ¥500–2,000
Loose Leaf Tea
High-quality sencha, hojicha (roasted), genmaicha (rice tea). Ippodo in Kyoto has been selling tea since 1717. Tsuen Tea in Uji is the oldest teahouse in the world (est. 1160!).
📍 Ippodo (Kyoto), Tsuen Tea (Uji), Itohkyuemon
💰 ¥500–3,000
Matcha Latte Sticks
Budget
Instant matcha latte powder sticks by Tsujiri or Itoen. Just add hot water. 10-20 sticks per box — super easy to carry and universally liked as a gift.
📍 Don Quijote, supermarkets, konbini
💰 ¥300–800

🎎 Traditional Crafts & Goods
Tenugui Cotton Towels
Traditional thin cotton cloths with beautiful prints — use as towels, wrapping cloth, wall art, or bandana. Light to carry and uniquely Japanese. Dozens of gorgeous patterns.
📍 Naramachi shops, Nishiki Market, Tokyu Hands
💰 ¥500–1,500
Chopsticks & Chopstick Rests
Great Gift
Beautifully crafted lacquerware or wooden chopsticks, often sold in pairs for couples. Ceramic chopstick rests shaped as animals, food, or seasonal motifs. Packaged in gift boxes.
📍 Nishiki Market, Kiyomizu shops, Naramachi
💰 ¥500–3,000
Kiyomizu-yaki Ceramics
Premium
Hand-painted Kyoto pottery — tea cups, sake sets, small plates. The shops along Kiyomizu-dera slope are full of them. Ranges from affordable to museum-quality pieces.
📍 Kiyomizu-zaka slope, Ninenzaka, Gion craft shops
💰 ¥800–10,000+
Furoshiki Wrapping Cloth
Traditional fabric squares for wrapping gifts, bottles, or bento. Reusable, eco-friendly, and beautiful. Doubles as a scarf or bag. Many stunning patterns available.
📍 Tokyu Hands, dept. stores, Kyoto craft shops
💰 ¥500–3,000
Nara Deer Goods
Budget Cute
Deer-themed everything: keychains, cookies, socks, pouches, magnets. Nara's deer are the mascot and the souvenir shops are packed with adorable deer merch.
📍 Nara Park area shops, Kintetsu Nara station
💰 ¥200–1,500
Omamori Charms
Meaningful
Protective amulets from temples/shrines — for health, love, study, travel, etc. Each shrine has unique designs. NOT sold in shops; only at the shrines themselves. Meaningful souvenirs.
📍 Fushimi Inari, Kiyomizu-dera, Todai-ji, Kasuga Taisha
💰 ¥500–1,000
Goshuin Stamp Book (御朱印帳)
Collectible calligraphy stamps from each temple/shrine visited. Buy a blank goshuin-cho (stamp book) at your first temple and collect stamps at each stop. A living travel diary.
📍 Any major temple or shrine (¥300 per stamp)
💰 ¥1,500–2,500 for book + ¥300/stamp
🥃 Drinks & Alcohol
Japanese Whisky
Premium Gift
Suntory Toki, Hibiki, Yamazaki, Nikka From The Barrel — globally acclaimed. Airport duty-free often has exclusives. Don Quijote has surprising stock at good prices. Check airline liquid limits!
📍 Don Quijote, KIX Airport duty-free
💰 ¥2,000–15,000+
Sake (日本酒)
Nada (Kobe) is Japan's top sake-producing region. Visit a brewery in Nada-Gogō district or pick up Kobe-brewed sake in Sannomiya. Dassai and Kubota are premium crowd-pleasers.
📍 Nada breweries (Kobe), Don Quijote, dept. food halls
💰 ¥1,000–5,000
Craft Beer
Regional
Minoh Beer (Osaka's pride — especially the W-IPA and Stout), Kyoto Brewing Co., Nara Beer. Cans are easy to pack and taste like the trip.
📍 Craft beer shops, Takamura Coffee (also beer!), konbini
💰 ¥400–800 per can
